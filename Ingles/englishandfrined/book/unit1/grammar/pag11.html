It's (about/high) time + subject + past simple
  • We use this expression to say that something we consider important or necesary is not happening.
  • It express complaint or criticism. You can use (it's time + to + infinitive) or (it's time for + subject + to + infinitive) but we lose the idea of criticism:
    • It's time to wade up (You need to wake up now)
    • It's time for her retire (she is old enough or she deserves it)
  • We use the pas simple but this expression refers to the presnet:
    • It's time you started studying for your exam (you're not studying but you should)
  • It's about time and it's high time are more formal and emphatic:
    • It's about time we took a holiday (whe haven't taken a holiday in a long time but we should)
    • It's high time you started studying for your final exams(you should start studying)

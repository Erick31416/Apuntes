The Present Perfect Simple is used:
  • a. for actions which happened in the past but the exact time is not mentioned.
  • b. for recently completed actions.
  • c. for past actions whose result are obvious in the present.
  • d. for actions which started in the past and continue up to the present(emphasis on the action).
  • e. with adjectives in the superlative form and expresions like the only/ first/second... etc.
The Present Perfect Progressive is used:
  • a for actions which started in the past and continue up to the present (emphasis on the duration of the action)
  • b for actions which happened over a period of time in the past and may have finished, but tht resutl are ovious in the present.
  • c to show annoyance resulting from a recent action.
